2. Niche and fitness differences

2.1. Making the background

Modern coexistence theory (Chesson 2000; summarized in Kraft) details the condition for pairwise coexistence:

\[ \rho<\frac{\kappa_j}{\kappa_i}<\frac{1}{\rho}, \]

where \(\rho\) is the pairwise niche overlap (its complement, \(1-\rho\), is the pairwise niche difference) and \(\kappa_i/\kappa_j\) is the average fitness difference between species \(i\) and \(j\). If we arbitrarily assign species \(j\) as the superior competitor, this reduces to: \[ \rho<\frac{\kappa_i}{\kappa_j}. \] Note that the subscripts on the kappas have flipped.

In the Sedgwick annual plant community, Kraft estimated these by fitting experimental data to a model of annual plant population dynamics with pairwise, Beverton-Holt competition terms.

Weâll begin by making the figure background to show which fitness differences (y-axis) are compatable with coexistence for each value of niche difference (x-axis). ggplot constructs plots as layers, and this is exactly how weâll reproduce Kraft Fig. 2.

2.2. Calculate all pairwise niche and fitness differences

Kraft calculated the pairwise niche overlap for annual plants as:

\[ \rho=\sqrt{\frac{\alpha_{ij}\alpha_{ji}}{\alpha_{jj}\alpha_{ii}}}, \] where \(\alpha_{ji}\) is the per-capita effect of species \(j\) on the fitness of species \(i\).

We can calculate this quantity for each species pair using the data in the alphas matrix as follows:

Kraft calculated the average fitness difference for each species pair as: \[ \begin{aligned} \frac{\kappa_j}{\kappa_i} &= \left(\frac{\eta_j-1}{\eta_i-1}\right)\sqrt{\frac{\alpha_{ij}\alpha_{ii}}{\alpha_{jj}\alpha_{ji}}} \\ &= \left(\frac{\frac{g_j\lambda_j}{1-(1-g_j)(s_j)}-1}{\frac{g_i\lambda_i}{1-(1-g_i)(s_i)}-1}\right)\sqrt{\frac{\alpha_{ij}\alpha_{ii}}{\alpha_{jj}\alpha_{ji}}}, \end{aligned} \] where \(g\) is the germination fraction, \(\lambda\) is the per-capita seed production of plants in the absence of competitors, and \(s_i\) is the annual survival rate of ungerminated seeds in the soil seedbank.

As before, we can calculate this quantity for all species pairs using the alphas matrix and the demog data frame.
